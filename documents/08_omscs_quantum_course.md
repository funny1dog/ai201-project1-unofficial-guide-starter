# r/OMSCS: Reviews on CS 8803 - Introduction to Quantum Computing

**User: grad_student_atl**
Thinking about taking CS 8803 (Intro to Quantum Computing) next semester. How is the workload? Is it all physics?

**Comment: OMSCS_Veteran**
I took it last Fall. It is a fantastic course, but brutally hard if your linear algebra is rusty. The first three weeks are pure math: Hilbert spaces, tensor products, and unitary matrices. If you don't know what a complex conjugate transpose is, review it before the semester starts. Workload was about 15-20 hours a week for me.

**Comment: python_coder**
The programming assignments are mostly in Python using Jupyter Notebooks, but they aren't traditional coding. You are usually building circuits to implement algorithms like Grover's or Simon's algorithm. The code itself is short (maybe 20 lines), but it will take you 10 hours of staring at a whiteboard to figure out what those 20 lines should be. 

**Comment: easy_A_hunter**
Do not take this as a filler class. The midterm average was a 62%. They curve heavily at the end, but you will feel like you are failing the entire semester. The professor is very active on Piazza, though, which is a huge plus.
