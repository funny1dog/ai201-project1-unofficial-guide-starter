# Hacker News: The Brutal Reality of Quantum Startups (Hype vs. Reality)

**Title:** IonQ, Rigetti, and the looming Quantum Winter

**Comment: tech_skeptic**
We are currently at the peak of the quantum hype cycle. Every startup promises fault-tolerant quantum computers within 5 years, but the physics just aren't cooperating. Qubit coherence times are still terrible. If you are joining a quantum startup today, realize you are joining a deep-tech research lab, not a SaaS company. 

**Comment: hardware_hacker**
I worked at one of the big superconducting qubit companies. The real bottleneck right now isn't even the quantum chips, it's the classical control hardware. We literally ran out of physical space in the dilution refrigerator for the coaxial cables needed to control 100+ qubits. Until we solve the wiring problem, scaling to 10,000 qubits is a pipe dream.

**Comment: q_investor**
Don't completely discount the NISQ (Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum) era. Yes, fault tolerance is a decade away, but companies are finding clever ways to use noisy qubits for quantum-inspired optimization algorithms today. It's not breaking RSA encryption, but it's useful for supply chain routing.

**Comment: burnt_out_dev**
The culture at these startups is wild. You have brilliant post-docs who treat software engineering like a suggestion, and seasoned SWEs who get frustrated because the hardware changes every two weeks, breaking all their APIs. It's a fun environment, but extremely stressful.
